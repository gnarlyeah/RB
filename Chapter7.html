Chapter 7: The Gift

I once knew a woman, strict and severe
A former nun, her faith was most dear
She lived her life with morals and grace
And expected the same of all in her place
    
I was drawn to her, and sought her out
For I had questions, doubts and more to discuss
We sat and talked for hours on end
Exchanging ideas, debating and comprehending
    
Her philosophy was grounded in her faith
And though it differed from mine, I couldn't hate
For she spoke with such conviction and love
That I couldn't help but be moved
    
And so I listened and learned from her teachings
Grateful for the wisdom she was willing to share
I left our conversations feeling uplifted and inspired
By that religious, conservative woman, forever admired.
